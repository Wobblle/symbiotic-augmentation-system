The Haptic Cognition Language (HCL)

HCL is structured like a language, with basic sensory units ("phonemes") combining to form meaningful concepts ("morphemes") and complex messages ("sentences").

Haptic "Phonemes": The Building Blocks

These are the most basic distinguishable qualities of vibration.

Intensity (Amplitude)

Frequency (Texture)

Directional Movement

A "wave" moving across the LRA array (e.g., left-to-right) to signify progress or valence.

Example "Sentence"

Combining primitives creates nuanced meaning.

Category: `Cognitive/Internal` (A brief, crisp hum on wrist).

Action: `Focus on Item #7` (A strong pulse on LRA #7).

Suggestion: `Consider Further` (A steady, sustained hum).

Full Interpretation:

"The AI suggests focusing on a specific thought or option, labeled as #7, as it may be the solution."
